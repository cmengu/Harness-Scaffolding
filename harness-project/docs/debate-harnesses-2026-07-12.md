# Multi-agent debate: research, harnesses, guardrails — survey vs council (12 Jul 2026)

All sources below were fetched and read on 12 Jul 2026 (arXiv page/full text, official docs, repo READMEs, GitHub API for stars/dates). Claims not verifiable this way are marked **UNVERIFIED**.

## TL;DR

1. The literature's debate protocols are short: Du = 2 rounds default, MAD ≤ 3, Khan = 3; every paper reports flat-or-worse returns past ~2–4 rounds. Council's `rounds=1` default is evidence-aligned.
2. Anthropic's own debate program found models "failing to engage with their debate opponents" and lacking multi-round coherence — they cut interaction entirely (concurrent opening statements). Cross-critique is the hard part, not the obvious part.
3. The strongest judge-bias fix in the literature — judge every debate **twice with answer order swapped, then average** (Khan et al.) — appears in **no** general framework and no Claude skill. Council does a single random shuffle + blind labels: halfway there.
4. Sycophancy is documented as a trained-in behavior (Sharma et al., Anthropic): humans and preference models sometimes prefer convincing sycophantic answers over correct ones. Only one 26-star community skill attempts detection (position changes without new evidence).
5. MAD's judge favored answers from its own model family 120:77 — council's cross-family heads + Claude-as-judge inherit exactly this risk when the judge is armed.
6. AutoGen/LangGraph/CrewAI ship turn order, shared-vs-isolated context, and termination conditions — and **zero epistemic guardrails** (no bias, sycophancy, or diversity machinery). Orchestration is commodity; epistemics are hand-rolled everywhere.
7. The Claude-skills debate ecosystem is young (all repos 2026, ≤65 stars), prose-not-code (guardrails are instructions to the model, unenforced), but conceptually sharp: decorrelated model families, blind scoring, theatrical-consensus flags, falsification conditions.
8. Council's ops guardrails (retry/quarantine, dead-head markers, append-only ledger) exceed every research repo surveyed — research code assumes calls succeed.
9. Council's real gaps, in order: capitulation/sycophancy signal → both-orders judging → hard duel budget cap → forced-disagreement pressure. Gap table at the end.

## 1. Research lineage (primary sources)

**Irving, Christiano & Amodei, "AI safety via debate" (arXiv 1805.00899, 2018)** — [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.00899)
Protocol: two agents in zero-sum self-play "take turns making short statements up to a limit, then a human judges which of the agents gave the most true, useful information." Ends at the statement limit; the human judge is the whole point (training honest behavior via a judge who can't verify directly). Toy result: debate over sparse MNIST pixels lifts a 6-pixel judge-classifier 59.4%→88.9% (4-pixel: 48.2%→85.2%). Self-reported limits: open empirical questions about human judges and whether honesty wins as capability scales.

**Du, Li, Torralba, Tenenbaum & Mordatch, "Improving Factuality and Reasoning in Language Models through Multiagent Debate" (arXiv 2305.14325, ICML 2024)** — [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14325) · [full text](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2305.14325) · [code, 543★](https://github.com/composable-models/llm_multiagent_debate)
Protocol: N symmetric agents (default **3 agents, 2 rounds**); each round every agent receives the other agents' full answers concatenated into its context and updates its own; the final answer is the common answer the population converges to ("the population almost always converges"). Self-reported failure modes: agents "confidently affirm that their answer is correct" even when the consensus is wrong; context grows until models "only focused on the most recent generations" (they summarize other answers to cope); prompt length is a hidden agreeableness knob — a "short debate prompt induces faster consensus"; models don't express uncertainty. Returns rise through ~4 rounds then plateau; cost scales with agents×rounds. Repo implements the four tasks with **no** termination/retry/cost/bias machinery.

**Liang et al., "Encouraging Divergent Thinking in Large Language Models through Multi-Agent Debate" (MAD, arXiv 2305.19118, EMNLP 2024)** — [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.19118) · [full text](https://arxiv.org/html/2305.19118) · [code, 597★](https://github.com/Skytliang/Multi-Agents-Debate)
Protocol: asymmetric roles — affirmative proposes, negative must disagree ("You disagree with the affirmative side's points"), alternating turns under a "tit for tat" meta-prompt; a **judge runs in two modes**: discriminative (after each round, decide whether to stop) and extractive (forced pick at the round cap, typically 3). Names **Degeneration-of-Thought**: once an LLM "has established confidence in its answers, it is unable to generate novel thoughts later through self-reflection even if the initial stance is incorrect" — debate exists to break DoT. Self-reported failures: disagreement must be **modest** — forced maximal disagreement (0.988 disagreement rate) underperforms the default level, as does forced consensus; adaptive termination is required; and "LLMs might not be a fair judge if different LLMs are used" — a GPT-4 judge preferred GPT-4-side answers **120 vs 77** against GPT-3.5 debaters. Repo: shell/py scripts, no cost/retry/bias guardrails documented.

**Chan et al., "ChatEval: Towards Better LLM-based Evaluators through Multi-Agent Debate" (arXiv 2308.07201, ICLR 2024)** — [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.07201) · [full text](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2308.07201) · [code, 340★](https://github.com/chanchimin/ChatEval)
Protocol: a referee **team** debates to judge text, not to answer. Three communication topologies: one-by-one (sequential, each sees prior speakers — best in the ChatGPT setting), simultaneous-talk (async rounds), simultaneous-talk-with-summarizer (an extra LLM compresses each round). Verdicts aggregate by majority vote (pairwise) or averaged scores. Self-reported failures: **homogeneous role prompts underperform and can fail to beat a single agent** — "diverse role prompts are necessary"; performance peaks at 3–4 agents and declines at 5; more discussion turns show "no significant upward trend" (context length blamed). Applies position calibration, citing Wang et al. 2023, "Large Language Models are not Fair Evaluators."

**Khan et al., "Debating with More Persuasive LLMs Leads to More Truthful Answers" (arXiv 2402.06782, ICML 2024 best paper)** — [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.06782) · [full text](https://arxiv.org/html/2402.06782) · [code, 131★](https://github.com/ucl-dark/llm_debate)
Protocol: information asymmetry on QuALITY — two expert debaters (full passage access) argue **assigned opposing answers** for 3 rounds of simultaneous 150-word statements (~900-word transcripts); a non-expert judge (no passage) picks. Quotes pass a verifier tool (`<v_quote>`/`<u_quote>` tags). Judge bias is handled head-on: "we run judgements over each debate twice — once with the original transcript and once with the debater order swapped," averaging both orders; persuasiveness measured by Elo over tournaments (+ best-of-N / critique-and-refinement debater optimization in the repo). Results: debate lifts LLM judges to 76% and humans to 88% (naive: 48%/60%); more persuasive debaters ⇒ more truthful judgments. Self-reported failures: without quote verification the dishonest debater "could simply create an alternative narrative"; weaker debaters fabricate quotes; LLM judges are overconfident and position-biased even after mitigation.

**Anthropic-published lineage.** (i) *Constitutional AI* ([arXiv 2212.08073](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073)): single-model self-critique→revision finetuning + RLAIF — critique without a second head. (ii) *Towards Understanding Sycophancy in Language Models* (Sharma et al., [arXiv 2310.13548](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.13548)): five SOTA assistants consistently match user beliefs over truth; cause traced to preference data — "both humans and preference models prefer convincingly-written sycophantic responses over correct ones a non-negligible fraction of the time." This is the trained-in prior any cross-critique protocol fights. (iii) *Anthropic Fall 2023 Debate Progress Update* (Radhakrishnan, [Alignment Forum, Nov 2023](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/QtqysYdJRenWFeWc4/anthropic-fall-2023-debate-progress-update)): earlier attempts hit models "failing to engage with their debate opponents and generally lacking the coherence needed for multiple rounds of arguments," so they switched to **concurrent opening statements** — debaters never see each other, arguing against a hypothetical opponent — with a supervised Claude 2 judge, self-play RL on debate wins, hidden-passage QuALITY and verified quotes. Verdict: "promise, in a simple and constrained setting."

## 2. Framework / harness implementations

**AutoGen (Microsoft)** — [termination docs](https://microsoft.github.io/autogen/stable/user-guide/agentchat-user-guide/tutorial/termination.html) · [SelectorGroupChat](https://microsoft.github.io/autogen/stable/user-guide/agentchat-user-guide/selector-group-chat.html) · [debate pattern](https://microsoft.github.io/autogen/stable/user-guide/core-user-guide/design-patterns/multi-agent-debate.html)
Primitives: teams with fixed turn order (`RoundRobinGroupChat`) or an LLM selector choosing the next speaker from names/descriptions (`SelectorGroupChat`, overridable via `selector_func`, `candidate_func`, `allow_repeated_speaker`); messages are **broadcast — every participant sees the full shared conversation** (context bleed is the default). Termination = 11 composable (AND/OR) conditions: MaxMessage, TextMention ("TERMINATE"), TokenUsage, Timeout, Handoff, SourceMatch, External, StopMessage, TextMessage, FunctionCall, Functional. A dedicated core design pattern implements sparse-topology multiagent debate (4 solvers on a square, each seeing 2 neighbors, 3 fixed rounds, aggregator majority vote — per the page it follows arXiv 2406.11776, **UNVERIFIED** — paper itself not fetched). Not guarded: no position-bias, sycophancy, convergence-quality, or dollar-cost machinery; debate termination in the pattern is a fixed round count.

**LangGraph / LangChain** — [multi-agent docs](https://docs.langchain.com/oss/python/langchain/multi-agent)
Primitives: patterns, not protocols — subagents (coordinator routes, per-agent context isolation), handoffs (peer transfer via `Command`, incl. `Command.PARENT` across subgraphs), skills, router, custom LangGraph workflow. The docs' organizing concern is "context engineering — deciding what information each agent sees." The multi-agent page offers **no termination/loop-limit guidance at all**; stop conditions, bias handling, and budgets are explicitly the developer's problem. No debate primitive.

**CrewAI** — [process docs](https://docs.crewai.com/concepts/processes)
Two processes: sequential (task outputs chain forward) and hierarchical (an auto-created manager LLM "oversees task execution, including planning, delegation, and validation"). The documented surface has no debate/consensus primitive, no round, bias, or budget controls.

**Dedicated harnesses with adoption.** The four paper repos above (131–597★) are experiment code: fixed protocols, no ops guardrails. The one big-adoption deliberation harness is **karpathy/llm-council** ([repo](https://github.com/karpathy/llm-council), 22,557★): 3 stages — all models answer independently → each **ranks the others' answers with identities anonymized** "so that the LLM can't play favorites" → a chairman synthesizes. Self-described "99% vibe coded" weekend project; no termination/budget/escalation machinery.

**Debby's debate SKILL.md** (local: `reference/omnigent/examples/debby/skills/debate/SKILL.md`) is council's direct ancestor: default 1 round; always cross answers ("each partner critiques the other's round N-1 answer — never its own"); moderator is "not a third debater"; prose early-stop ("if two rounds produce no new movement… converge early"); inspect a dead partner's history rather than silently dropping a voice. Council turned these instructions into code.

**Reading of the ecosystem:** skills encode genuinely sharp guardrail *ideas*, but as prose obligations on the orchestrating model — nothing enforces the blind scoring or the sycophancy flag. Council's differentiator is that its guardrails are deterministic code with ledger provenance.

## 4. Guardrails inventory (who implements what)

- **(a) Judge position/order bias** — Literature: judge twice with order swapped + average (Khan); position calibration citing Wang et al. (ChatEval); model-favoritism measured 120:77 (MAD); identity anonymization (llm-council, council-review, agent-review-panel blind scoring). Frameworks: **none** (AutoGen/LangGraph/CrewAI have no concept of it). The literature's strongest fix (both-orders averaging) exists in **no** surveyed harness or skill.
- **(b) Sycophancy/capitulation detection** — Literature: cause established (Sharma et al.); MAD shows the inverse failure too (DoT = refusing to move) and that *modest* disagreement beats forced extremes; Du shows prompt length silently modulates agreeableness. Implementations: only agent-review-panel (position-change-without-new-evidence flag) and adversarial-panel (forced disagreement) — prose-only. Frameworks: none.
- **(c) Convergence/early-stop** — Literature: adaptive stop required (MAD discriminative judge — an LLM call, hence biased); returns plateau ~2–4 rounds (Du), no upward trend with turns (ChatEval), fixed 3 rounds (Khan). Implementations: AutoGen's 11 termination conditions (syntactic, not semantic); alecnielsen circuit breaker; council-review KS-statistic; Debby prose rule.
- **(d) Budget caps** — Khan's per-round word limits are in-protocol budgets; AutoGen TokenUsage/Timeout termination; agent-review-panel dollar estimates; council-review call-count modes. Hard dollar caps: essentially nowhere.
- **(e) Role/context separation** — Literature: information asymmetry *by design* (Khan/Anthropic hidden passage); diverse personas necessary (ChatEval). Frameworks: AutoGen group chat **broadcasts everything** (bleed by default); LangChain subagents isolate. Skills: decorrelated model families (adversarial-panel ladder, llm-council).
- **(f) Escalation-to-human** — Irving's frame makes the human judge load-bearing; Khan uses human judges as the gold standard. Implementations: agent-review-panel's low-confidence flag is the only skill with an explicit path; frameworks offer mechanisms (AutoGen HandoffTermination) but no policy.
- **(g) Diversity preservation** — Homogeneous agents fail (ChatEval); cross-model decorrelation ("blind spots… from the same weights… are correlated" — adversarial-panel; anonymized cross-family council — llm-council); theatrical-consensus and correlated-bias flags (council-review, agent-review-panel); modest tit-for-tat (MAD).

**Structural gap:** frameworks supply turn order, context topology, and stop conditions; *every* epistemic guardrail (a, b, f, g) lives only in papers and tiny prose skills. Anything council enforces in code here is ahead of the field's tooling.

## Council gap table

Grounded in `council/debate.py`, `config.py`, `shadow.py` (commit-state 12 Jul 2026):

| Guardrail | Council state | Evidence in code / gap |
|---|---|---|
| (a) Position/order bias | **Partial** | `_synthesize`: blind labels + `random.shuffle(pair)` + `judge_keymap` ledger row — one random order per verdict. Missing: Khan's judge-twice-with-swapped-order-and-average (2 judge calls). |
| (b) Sycophancy/capitulation | **Missing** | `_moved` measures churn, not concession quality: wholesale adoption of the other head's answer = high movement (round continues), then converges next round unflagged. No new-evidence test on position changes (cf. agent-review-panel). |
| (c) Convergence/early-stop | **Implemented** | `_moved(prev, now) < 0.10` on *both* heads → `converged` ledger event; deterministic (no biased LLM stop-judge, unlike MAD). Caveats: surface-textual diff; never fires at default `rounds=1`. |
| (d) Budget caps | **Partial** | `rounds=1` default, `head_timeout=300`, `head_retries=2`, `ask_budget_usd` = soft nag only (`code_budget_usd` hard-gates code mode). Missing: hard token/$ stop for a duel; Khan-style per-round word limits. |
| (e) Role/context separation | **Implemented** | Two model families in separate subprocesses w/ own native sessions; human-confirmed briefing gate before the adversary sees anything; `_CRIT_INSTR` forces standalone answers; `_is_dead` keeps error text from becoming debate content. Stronger than AutoGen's broadcast default; real model separation, not persona theater. |
| (f) Escalation-to-human | **Implemented** (when judge armed) | `reasoning` judge may open ESCALATE → `DebateResult.escalated` → banner; judge OFF by default = the human *is* the judge (Irving-consistent); Phase-4 gate exits 1 on escalate. |
| (g) Diversity preservation | **Partial** | Cross-family heads = structural decorrelation (the skills' top rung). Missing: MAD-style modest forced-disagreement pressure; `_CRIT_INSTR`'s "incorporating whatever the debate conceded" nudges toward consensus with no theatrical-consensus check. |
| Ops (retry/quarantine/dead heads) | **Implemented, ahead of field** | `_safe`/`_safe_stream`: classify transient vs permanent, backoff retries, quarantine postmortems, session-clear on death, `_is_dead` corpse guard, append-only ledger, shadow A/B config diff. None of the four paper repos document any of this. |

**Cheapest high-value moves per the evidence:** (1) both-orders judging in `_synthesize` (+1 judge call, directly Khan-backed); (2) a capitulation heuristic — flag when a head's round-N answer moves *toward* the other head's round-(N−1) answer more than some threshold without citing new evidence, as a ledger row not a block; (3) a hard duel budget knob; (4) A/B-test a "you need not agree; find the strongest genuine disagreement first" line in `_CRIT_INSTR` via shadow mode (MAD: modest > maximal).
